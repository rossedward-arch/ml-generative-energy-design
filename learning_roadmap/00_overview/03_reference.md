## 1. Recommended Books & Textbooks

### 1.1 Python Programming & Data Science

-   **"Python Crash Course"** by Eric Matthes
    * **Why:** A beginner-friendly introduction to Python fundamentals with practical projects, ideal for foundational programming skills.
    * **Suggested Sections:** Chapters 1â€“10 covering basics, control flow, functions, and file I/O.

-   **"Automate the Boring Stuff with Python"** by Al Sweigart
    * **Why:** Excellent for practical scripting and automating everyday tasks, useful for workflow efficiency and quick wins.
    * **Suggested Sections:** Chapters on file handling, web scraping, and working with Excel/CSV files.

-   **"Python for Data Analysis"** by Wes McKinney
    * **Why:** The definitive guide to NumPy and Pandas, essential libraries for data manipulation and analysis in ML workflows.
    * **Suggested Sections:** Chapters on data structures, data cleaning, data loading, and time series.

### 1.2 Statistics & Data Science Foundations

-   **"Think Stats"** by Allen B. Downey
    * **Why:** Introduces core statistical concepts using Python examples, providing a solid foundation in probability and statistical reasoning.
    * **Suggested Sections:** Chapters on distributions, hypothesis testing, and regression.

-   **"Practical Statistics for Data Scientists"** by Peter Bruce and Andrew Bruce
    * **Why:** Covers essential statistics concepts tailored specifically for data science applications, bridging theory and practical implementation.
    * **Suggested Sections:** Chapters on exploratory data analysis, sampling, hypothesis testing, and regression.

-   **"Statistics Done Wrong"** by Alex Reinhart *(Free online PDF)*
    * **Why:** Critically examines common pitfalls and errors in statistical analysis, improving understanding of valid interpretation and avoiding mistakes.
    * **Suggested Sections:** Recommended to read entirely for a deeper appreciation of statistical rigor.

### 1.3 Machine Learning Fundamentals

-   **"Hands-On Machine Learning with Scikit-Learn, Keras & TensorFlow"** by AurÃ©lien GÃ©ron
    * **Why:** A highly practical, comprehensive guide to machine learning and deep learning, progressing from fundamental algorithms to advanced concepts with real datasets.

-   **"Introduction to Machine Learning with Python"** by Andreas C. MÃ¼ller

    * **Why:** Provides a comprehensive overview of practical methods to interpret and explain machine learning models, crucial for building trustworthy AI in research and practice.
    * **Suggested Sections:** Chapters on model-agnostic explanations (e.g., LIME, SHAP values), feature importance, and visualization techniques.

### 1.4 Reinforcement Learning

-   **"Deep Learning"** by Ian Goodfellow, Yoshua Bengio, and Aaron Courville
    * **Why:** A comprehensive and rigorous textbook on deep learning, ideal for understanding the theoretical underpinnings of neural networks and advanced ML architectures.
    * **Suggested Sections:** Chapters on linear algebra, probability, deep feedforward networks, and regularization.

-   **"Deep Learning with Python"** by FranÃ§ois Chollet
    * **Why:** A highly practical and code-focused guide to deep learning using Keras, enabling rapid prototyping and understanding of deep learning applications.
    * **Suggested Sections:** Chapters on deep learning basics, convolutional networks, and recurrent neural networks.

### 1.6 Generative Design Techniques

-   **"Design Exploration for Generative Design"** by Asterios Agkathidis
    * **Why:** Covers fundamental concepts, methodologies, and workflows in architectural generative design and optimization.
    * **Suggested Sections:** Introductory chapters on parametric workflows, evolutionary algorithms, and optimization strategies in architecture.

-   **"The Nature of Code"** by Daniel Shiffman *(Free online book with p5.js examples)*
    * **Why:** Explores computational approaches to natural systems, including evolutionary algorithms and autonomous agents, highly relevant for algorithmic design approaches.
    * **Suggested Sections:** Chapters on genetic algorithms, neural networks, and autonomous agents.

### 1.7 Energy Simulation & Building Performance

-   **"The Passivhaus Designer's Manual: A Technical Guide to Low and Zero Energy Buildings"** by Janet Cotterell and Adam Dadeby
    * **Why:** A comprehensive and practical guide to Passivhaus standards and low-energy building design strategies, suitable for detailed technical reference.
    * **Suggested Sections:** Chapters on thermal performance, ventilation systems, airtightness, and detailed design strategies.

-   **"Energy Simulation in Building Design"** by Joseph Clarke
    * **Why:** An in-depth technical book that links building energy simulation theory with real-world design workflows, ideal for advanced understanding of simulation principles.
    * **Suggested Sections:** Chapters on modeling principles, simulation validation, and case studies.

-   **"Net Zero Energy Buildings"** by Linda Reeder
    * **Why:** Provides a good introduction and overview of high-performance and net-zero energy building design concepts, offering broader context.
    * **Suggested Sections:** Chapters on definitions, strategies, and case studies for achieving net-zero energy.

### 1.8 Application Development & UI

-   **"Streamlit for Data Science"** by Tyler Richards *(Free online book)*
    * **Why:** A practical guide to building interactive data applications and dashboards quickly using Streamlit, invaluable for prototyping research tools and sharing results.
    * **Suggested Sections:** Recommended to read entirely for rapid application development skills.
